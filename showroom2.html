Forex Trading Concepts

Doji Candle

The Doji candlestick pattern signifies indecision between buyers and sellers. When the opening and closing prices are nearly the same, it suggests a balance between the forces of supply and demand. This pattern often signals that a reversal may be imminent, especially in the context of an established trend.

Market Sessions

The forex market is open 24 hours a day, and it is divided into four major trading sessions: Sydney, Tokyo, London, and New York. Each session has its own market dynamics, with varying levels of liquidity, volatility, and trading activity. The overlap of sessions, especially between London and New York, is when the market is most active and presents higher trading opportunities.

Support & Resistance

Support and resistance levels are fundamental concepts in technical analysis. Support is a price level where demand is strong enough to prevent further decline, while resistance is a level where selling pressure halts upward movement. These levels help traders pinpoint key price zones for potential buy or sell opportunities and are often used to identify trend reversals or trend continuation signals.

Trendlines

Trendlines are a vital tool in technical analysis, used to connect price highs in a downtrend or price lows in an uptrend. These lines act as dynamic levels of support or resistance and help traders visualize the direction of the market. By assessing the angle and position of the trendline, traders can gauge the strength of the trend and make informed decisions on potential breakouts or trend continuation.
